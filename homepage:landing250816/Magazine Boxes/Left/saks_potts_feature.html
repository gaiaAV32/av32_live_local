The Colour-Block Coat

WHO: Spotted at fashion week on Instagram’s Head of Fashion Partnerships Eva Chen, Man Repeller’s Leandra Medine, and Glossier’s Emily Weiss: you’ll want to swaddle yourself in this teddy coat come winter. As practical as it is eye-catching, it’ll happily do drinks, the grocery shop, and fashion week too.

HOW: Celebrate those bold lapels and flashes of colour and clash with all your might. A red dress or a contrasting cricket jumper will do nicely, or make like Chen and grab a brightly-clad friend.

Do it like a Dane – or specifically, Copenhagen-based Barbara Potts and Cathrine Saks. The duo’s womenswear designs are a tonic for a tired wardrobe, jazzing up jeans and bland neutral knits. A clever combination of coincidental colours, unusual textures, superior fabrics, avant-garde details and season-spanning shapes, any it-girl worth her following is investing in one of their catnip coats. And they are good coats – by which we mean warm, well-made, will-see-you-through-the-winter but also breathtakingly beautiful coats. Long gone are the days when fashion’s finest would shiver for the sake of a snap. Take inspiration from these street-style darlings and embrace your closet’s hearty new heroine.

The Tibetan Jacket

WHO: Scandinavians boast a keen eye when it comes to style – our enduring fascination with Freja Beha Erichsen, Caroline Brasch Nielsen and stylist Pernille Teisbaek is surely testament. A favourite of them all? A great big furry coat, in surprisingly seductive colour combinations.

HOW: Ochre and baby blue, who knew how well they’d go together? Pull over a party dress for nippy nights or layer with classic brown and accessories and a ’70s button down skirt.
