INSIDE THE MOST STYLISH SUITCASES

Meticulously planned (and folded) or thrown in a bag at the last minute, mastering the art of packing is easier said than done. We asked four fashionable frequent flyers how they do it:

Kim Hersov

fashion editor and Talitha co-founder based in London
 

“This summer I’m in India for Talitha, Mykonos and Lake Tahoe in my native California, which is beautiful and very relaxed. Prep is key for any trip: I spend time pulling out what I want to pack and then editing to make sure it all works together. I used to swim competitively and am a bikini and a one-piece girl – my brand of the moment is Marysia Swim. My vacation look is bohemian with a Californian twist.”

Gabriele Frantzen

Jewellery designer based in Munich
 

“I always pack a bikini, tunic, shorts, espadrilles, sandals, leather woven basket, coin purse and my vintage seahorse ring. Rolling means nothing is wrinkled upon arrival and saves space. A good vacation should leave you feeling beautiful, relaxed, and filled with sun, energy and love.”

Serafina Sama

Isa Arfen founder based in London
 

“I hate packing and always postpone the process until about four hours before my flight, then frantically throw my entire bedroom into a suitcase. My must-packs include black bikinis, white shirts and ’90s Levis cut-off jeans. I’ll be wearing vintage white dresses à la Bianca Jagger and handmade clogs when sightseeing. Good music, good friends, good food, some dancing and a lot of laughing are my idea of vacation heaven!”
