Linda Farrow’s son and Creative Director Simon Jablon
 

For designers looking to transfer their aesthetic from ready-to-wear to eyewear, working with Linda Farrow is a win-win for everyone involved. The luxury brand is an expert in the field, having crafted fashionable and pioneering frames since the 1970s. Now helmed by the founder’s son Simon Jablon and his wife Tracy Sedino, Linda Farrow is also renowned for its excellent quality and originality. This season’s collaborations with 3.1 Phillip Lim, Erdem and Yazbukey boldy reinforce all of the above with highly flattering and covetable results. Here we showcase our highlights and Jablon reveals the thinking behind them.

Q. Is it important for styles to represent both the designer and Linda Farrow’s creative philosophies?

 

A. Linda Farrow has its own design and material DNA. When it comes to the collaboration, it is purely a product that represents the designer and their world. The only thing that stays consistent through the various collaborations is the attention to detail and quality of the production. The knowledge that Linda Farrow over saw the construction and production means you will be buying a Phillip Lim design that is produced by Linda Farrow.

 
 

Q. How do collaborations come about?

A. Collaborations normally come about via friends or contacts within the industry. First and foremost is that we have to love the brand. Secondly, there has to be a point and a conceptual purpose to the collection (we don’t do anything just for the sake of it), and last of all but equally important, we need to have a good relationship with the designer and their team. Life is too short to not enjoy what you do.

Q. There are all cat-eye frames in all three collaborations: what is it about the specific shape?

 

A. It is a shape that for a large amount of people is very complimentary. Not only that it has become a bit of a trend and adding the two together means you will get more people jumping on that and following our lead.
