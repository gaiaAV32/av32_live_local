Q. Is it important for styles to represent both the designer and Linda Farrow’s creative philosophies?

 

A. Linda Farrow has its own design and material DNA. When it comes to the collaboration, it is purely a product that represents the designer and their world. The only thing that stays consistent through the various collaborations is the attention to detail and quality of the production.

 
 

Q. How do collaborations come about?

 

A. They normally come about via friends or contacts within the industry. First and foremost, we have to love the brand [to work with them]. Secondly, there has to be a point and a conceptual purpose to the collection – we don’t do anything just for the sake of it. And last but not least, we need to have a good relationship with the designer and their team. Life is too short to not enjoy what you do.

Q. There are all cat-eye frames in all three collaborations: what’s so great about that specific shape?

 

A. It is a shape that for a large amount of people is very complimentary. It has become a bit of a trend and adding the two together means you will get more people jumping on that and following our lead.
