MOST WANTED SHOES

Stride through Pre Fall 16 in style

 

Whether you’re a minimal flats kind of woman, or your tastes veer towards high-glamour, high heels, you’ll find a shoe that ticks all the boxes this season. Take your pick and stride through Pre Fall 16 in style.

 
 
 

Sonia Rykiel

 

WHO? The quintessential Left Bank cool designer, Sonia Rykiel opened her first boutique in 1968. Today finds Julie de Libran heading it up and reworking the house codes for now.
WHAT NOW? With more than a hint of ’70s glamour, the velvet jewel embellished platforms are the most fun you’ll have on your feet this season.
WEAR TO... These va-va-voom heels were made for girls who dance until dawn.

Paul Andrew

 

WHO? Having honed his craft at Alexander McQueen and Calvin Klein, Paul Andrew launched his namesake label in 2013.
WHAT NOW? Fashion insiders love Andrew’s flawless craftsmaship and sleek lines, both of which you’ll find on his signature Rhea flats, updated for now in scarlet suede.
WEAR TO... The perfect 9-9 pump, the Rheas will take you from day to date night in impeccable style.

 
 
 

Tabitha Simmons

 

WHO? Tabitha Simmons, the stylist-turned-editor-turned shoe designer, who’s made a name with her edgily elegant styles.
WHAT NOW? The Ginger shoes, which reimagine a ladylike silhouette with a chunky mid heel – a typical high/low Tabitha touch.
WEAR TO... That floral print was made for summer weddings (plus, the sturdy block heel holds up much better than stilettos on grass. Bonus).

Martiniano

 

WHO? Martiniano Lopez Crozet who spearheaded the trend for glove shoes with his line of ultra-soft, pared-back footwear all handmade in Argentina.
WHAT NOW? He might have coined the name, but Martiniano isn’t just about glove shoes; these ultra-simple lace-ups have the same restrained, minimal appeal.
WEAR TO... Consider these your sneaker upgrade for the season ahead. Simple.

 
 
 

Robert Clergerie

 

WHO? A master of masculine-meets-feminine chic, Robert Clergerie founded his eponymous brand in 1981. You’ll now find Roland Mouret at the helm.
WHAT NOW? The easy Alico backless loafers continue the androgynous tradition of the house.
WEAR TO... This slip-on style exudes effortless elegance. Play around with the boyish styling and team with a dress for a modernist’s take.
