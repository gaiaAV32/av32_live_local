COURREGES' NEXT CHAPTER

The rebirth of an iconic French brand

Courrèges’ next chapter

Arnaud Vaillant and Sébastien Meyer’s meteoric rise from buzzy newcomers to artistic directors at one of fashion’s most iconic houses wasn’t totally unexpected. The Paris based duo had already made a name for themselves with the modern minimalism of their own label Coperni Femme (now sadly discontinued). Now at the helm of Courrèges, they’ve tapped into the same space-age spirit, modern materials and super short hemlines the designer shook up the ’60s fashion scene with. (Fun fashion fact: André Courrèges is, along with Mary Quant, credited with popularising the mini skirt).

Vaillant and Meyer’s debut Spring Summer 2016 collection of 15 cuts in 15 colours in 15 materials represents an exciting re-imagination of Courrèges’ creative codes for the contemporary customer. The flattering archival fits - including some beautifully boxy jackets - layered on top of second skin bodies (another Courrèges signature), have the same streamlined and shrunken proportions that echo the ’60s aesthetic, but the detailing has developed for now. Besides the punchy palette and popper fastenings, shoulders and hemlines curve and leathers are patchworked together for a satisfyingly sci-fi effect. A perfect balance of statement and staple, there's no doubt these pieces have future classic written all over them.
