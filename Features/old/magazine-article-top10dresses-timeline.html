THE TOP 10: DRESSES

Shop AW15’s immediate updates

If you thought the dress would be side-lined in a season full of sensational separates then you are mistaken. In fact, the original outfit-in-one has been elevated beyond our wildest demands. From day into night, work to event, weekend and beyond: there is a highly prized dress to get you there...

Antonio Berardi has the sexy, sculptural section of your eveningwear wardrobe covered. This streamlined showstopper, with its touches of skin and asymmetric neckline, proves that orange really is the new black- but only after dark.

J.JS. Lee applies her 'cleanist' approach to knitwear, and in the shade of the season no less. Lithe in silhouette and funnel necked for more formality, you'll be warmly received at any smart-casual happening, and that includes the office.

Phillip Lim pushes his urban uniform into punk territory with punchy check prints and dangling D-ringed straps. This revamped shirt shape will take you far, regardless of accessories.

J.W. Anderson’s leather mini wraps then buckles a 1980s silhouette with stylish foresight. This is the decade to test out this season, plus the dress turns into a tactile tunic over trousers if you want to test the trend.

This Jill Stuart dress is definitely worth its weight in pinstriped gold when it comes to recapturing the shimmering spirit of Studio 54. Belt the waist, as styled in the show, or let it flow fully – the choice is yours...

Derek Lam dedicated his cinematic collection of ‘70s modern classics to actresses like Diane Keaton and Mia Farrow which reflected in the feminine strength, corseted rigour and offhand buttons of this runway piece. Wow at work with a shirt underneath.

Pedro Del Hierro’s leafy gown surprisingly nails night-to-day dressing. It may have dazzling, black tie intentions but this dress is just begging to grunged-down with heavy duty boots and a cool leather jacket.

Print master Jonathan Saunders’ colour combinations are just as renowned, and the modish simplicity of this A-line shape allows the shades to shine. It’s delightfully demure with scuba panelling to up the modernity.

Isa Arfen’s ladylike waist-whittler is a 1970s-meets-1990s fusion that is sartorial perfection. And its substantial winter-proof tweed will see you through the colder months.

Alexander Wang’s all black midi is a transformative triumph, despite its subcultural sentiments. It dresses up and down thanks to the decadent decorative details and layers so dreamily over a shirt or polo-neck.
