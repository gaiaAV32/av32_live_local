PARIS GOES PUNK

How to Rebel in Style

Ever since Hedi Slimane took the reigns at Saint Laurent in 2012, the ‘La Garconne’ look has topped the style charts. The androgynous anthesis to the pretty La Parisienne- think skinny tops and trousers, modern metal and lashings of leather; she’s edgier than before with rock’n’roll rebellion rippling from every mannish seam. Just like Patti Smith, one of Slimane’s musical muses and an Ann Demulemeester collaborator, who blazed this punkish ‘I don’t care what you think’ trail back in the 1970s. The iconic American singer, who releases her second memoir ‘M train’ this month, growled and prowled on stage in thrift store finds or clothes stolen from her late lover Robert Mapplethorpe.This is definitely the coolest way to dabble in the decade’s dresscodes...

Strike Gold

Parisians wear metallics dawn-to-dawn which always adds a scintillating sense of ‘je ne sais quoi’ to proceedings. Plus the pinstripes punctuating the glistening gold of Jill Stuart’s cuffed top give its girlish glamour a more graphic twist. Bold black accompaniments only intensify the insouciant effect.

The JUMP-START

When Parisians do causal, it’s still smart but the fit is invitingly relaxed. So they’d pull off Rachel Comey’s khaki jumpsuit, as janitor-like as it looks, with both brogues and heels. Its boyish brilliance lies in its urban utility, especially when topped with a trench.
