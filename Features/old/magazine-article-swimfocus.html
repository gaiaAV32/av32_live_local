MAKING A SPLASH

The swimwear designers to know.

When you feel like taking a dip, if you’re beach bound, or feel like a spot of sunbathing- a killer bikini or one-piece is a necessity all year round. Our swimwear specialists offer the classic and contemporary cuts you’ve been waiting for which should slip easily and stylishly into every lifestyle. Flattering fits and comfort come first of course, as they’ve taken your priorities on board, but styles also incorporate the ready-to-wear details you’ve been admiring too.

Lisa Marie Fernandez’s flirty styles bring 70s glamour bang up to date using innovative bonded and denim fabrics, classic shades and plenty of playful features. They’re named after fashionable fans like Poppy Delevingne and Mira Duma who wear them.

Kiinni totally transformed the triangle bikini with vivid crocheted trims, high tech fabrics and a strong bohemian spirit. The lack of fastenings ensures the most secure fit and to keep the winning formula fresh: new colourways have been introduced.

Mikoh keeps its sleek designs seamless and hardware free and you can pick an original support system as necklines are multi-strapped, braided, tasselled, racer back or strapless. Bold hues, prints and luxe fabrics are other distinguishing details.

Taking the plunge is pretty easy with Lilliput and Felix’s teasing bust lines, super sweet surface patterns and pastel palette. Tops are tied, wrapped or key holed to emphasise the skin on show while separates can be matched and clashed.

Marysia Swim’s charming scallop trim has always been a signature but the new multi-coloured stripes, developed in collaboration with artist Sally King Benedict; single shoulders, punchy tones and centre cutaways tap into the trends unwittingly too.

With Jane Birkin in mind, Bower taps into the sportier side of her sex appeal. Models may be minimal but the higher cut briefs, low fronts and backs expose skin in a mesmerising way using two straps or less.

Zimmermann have managed to squeeze in all the most romantic details- lace, ruffles and florals- to feminise their strong 50s inspired shapes. As super cute and as low cut as they are, the brand’s carefree sophistication isn’t lost.

Lazul deliberates the details to set its stunning styles apart. Hardware is bespoke and braided, the wiring invisible, and the high performance material feels ultra-luxe. The tan-popping tropical tones and prints take them up another glamourous notch.

Prism focuses on framing your favourite parts of your figure using a range of techniques. The high-waisted briefs whittle while they work, the U-shaped wiring around the bust make a very supportive statement and the geometric print looks great against every skin tone.

Roksanda aka the Queen of Colour, always combines shades in wonderful ways you’ve never considered before and the wine/cobalt/black/pink/orange she chose for her newest swimwear is a case in point. Sculpted shapes are another one of her hallmarks that have chicly crossed over from her RTW collections.
