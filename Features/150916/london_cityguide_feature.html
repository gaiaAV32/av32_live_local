FASHION WEEK CITY GUIDE: LONDON

 
Next up, London town. Often labelled the eccentric stop on the fashion week map, this is a city of true creatives, with more than one surprise planned for the catwalk we’re sure. Arriving from New York with the inevitable jet lag, it’s more important than ever to find home comforts, coffee and a great big comfortable bed to make dozing off in a different time zone a little easier. Below, find our round up of the best London has to offer.
 
 

For inspiration

Paper Journal at Webber Gallery Space

 
Head to online visual arts magazine Paper Journal’s third birthday exhibition to discover the hottest names in contemporary art and photography. Curated with past contributors from around the world to showcase their new work, expect plenty of intriguing visual delights.

For forty winks

Chiltern Firehouse

 
A still-happening haunt, you’ll probably end up at the Marylebone based hotel and eatery anyway so book a room and save on all those late night cab fares. Besides the inevitable A-lister sightings and food from Michelin-starred chef Nuno Mendes (those crab doughnuts really are worth the hype), you’ll also have a personal concierge at your beck and call.
 
www.chilternfirehouse.com
 
 

For a culture fix

Georgia O’Keeffe at the Tate Modern

 
Tate Modern’s retrospective of the American modernist's works range from her early paintings of New York to her masterpiece Jimson Weed. The perfect place to road test those AW16 florals, don’t miss the chance to take a look around the Tate’s newly opened £260m extension, the Switch House, either.
 
www.tate.org.uk

For a caffeine fix

Flat White

 
Located a stone's throw from Brewer Street Carpark, the hub of London Fashion Week, Flat White celebrates its 11th year bringing artisan coffee from Aus and NZ to Soho this September. It’s also Brioni creative director Justin O’Shea’s favourite coffee shop.
 
www.flatwhitesoho.co.uk
 
 

For the sweet tooth

Lily Vanilli

 
If you don’t already, follow Lily Vanilli’s mouthwatering Instagram quick (her 70s tea trolley for a Shrimps party is a highlight), and then head to her Columbia Rd café on a Sunday or to Fortnum’s any other day for one of her drop dead delicious brownies.
 
www.lilyvanilli.com

For a nightcap

Bronte

 
Newly opened Bronte on the Strand has high ceilings, beautiful Tom Dixon interiors, and a great big pink bar at which to sip cocktails long into the evening. (Did you get the memo? Pink is back in a big way this season).
 
www.bronte.co.uk
 
 

For an emergency blow-dry

Neil Cornelius

 
Look, we know what the weather is like here. For those with even a hint of a curl, England’s seemingly permanent drizzle can ruin the best of days. Whatever time, day or night, book a blow-dry at Neil Cornelius (who has catered to the tresses of Keira Knightley and the Duchess of Cambridge) and he’ll see you right.
 
www.neilcornelius.com

For brunch

The Wolseley

 
We’ve said it already but we’ll say it again. Places come and places go, but The Wolseley does the best eggs on earth. It’s London girl Kate Moss’ favourite too, and you can’t argue with that.
 
www.thewolseley.com
 
 

For people watching

Farmacy

 
Notting Hill’s answer to the #eatclean movement, this super healthy eatery has gained quite the fan base. Liv Tyler had her baby shower here, Stella McCartney’s a regular… Opt for an Earth Bowl or the veggie burger and enjoy feeling full and virtuous at the same time.
 
www.farmacylondon.com
