AVENUE 32’S SS17
FASHION MONTH TRENDS

NAIL THEM WITH OUR LUXURY WOMENSWEAR DESIGNERS

What better time is there than Fashion Month to shop your Spring Summer 2017 wish list? All the luxury designer womenswear collections you’ve been dreaming about since the last one are finally starting to arrive on the Avenue; and the rolling streetstyle feed should provide plenty of inspiration. After all, fashion editors, buyers and influencers, like our founder Roberta Benteler, are sure to be wearing the most coveted new season styles during five jam-packed weeks of shows in four fashion capitals. They try before you buy then we’ll ship your purchases worldwide, and to entice you even further, here’s our handy SS17 trend guide. Everything you need to know to master the key looks, from the go-to brands to Fashion Director Stephen Ayres’s expert advice, is below. And we’ve also teamed up with London Fashion Week Festival to bring them to life via a not-to-be missed trend catwalk later this month. Hope you can make it.

 
 

MAGIC METALLICS

SHOP NOW >

What: Making simple silhouettes spectacular with a whole host of eye-catching finishes, all that glitters isn’t just gold and silver this season. Pastels and paintbox shades also receive the metallic treatment, helping you shine in ingenious new ways.

Who: Haider Ackermann, Marco De Vincenzo, Galvan, Preen by Thornton Bregazzi, Racil, Camilla Elphick, Jerome Dreyfuss, Coliac, Martiniano, Minna Parikka and Women by Common Projects.

Stephen says: “Seen in all the big fashion shows- light reflecting textures and shiny surfaces are an unavoidable trend for SS17. The sparkling glow reflects a strong woman who wants to extenuate her style from day to night.”

Wear: Dress them down with sleek tailoring during office hours, yes even sequins, and denim come the weekend. After dark, release your inner magpie by matching and clashing these metallics to your heart’s content. You can’t beat the glamour.

PLEATS OR PLISSÉ?

SHOP NOW >

What: The debate continues as both fabric folds have been deployed to such wonderful, figure-skimming effect. Pleats are larger and firmly established as a fashion favourite, but plissé’s tiny, tasteful crinkles are enjoying a real comeback. No ironing needed here.

Who: Haider Ackermann, Rochas, Arthur Arbesser, Beaufille, Leur Logette.

Stephen says: “Pleats are a great way to elegantly wow in the work place. I love the feminine fluidity that they bring to a simple look without overshadowing the overall silhouette.”

Wear: You’ll find pleats and plissé on everything from entrance-making gowns to standout tops, terrific trousers and more with countless styling possibilities. Even the much-loved midi has been reimagined with exciting new pleat placements this season.

READY, SET, STRIPE!

SHOP NOW >

What: Stripes are a springtime stalwart that has undergone a dramatic upgrade. A minimal foil to more elaborate motifs no longer, the new line-up is loud and gloriously graphic with diagonal strokes proven to be surprisingly flattering.

Who: Sonia Rykiel, Roksanda, Marco de Vincenzo, FLOW the Label, Rosetta Getty, Jonathan Simkhai, Clu, LemLem, Leur Logette, Ace & Jig, Ganni, Haider Ackermann, Mira Mikati, Joshua Sanders, No.21, Gül Hürgel, Marco De Vincenzo, Sea Ny, Isabel Marant Étoile, Y/Project, Vivetta.

Stephen says: “This trend was hard to hide from, the epitome of spring summer, and one we happily bought into. Whether applied to silk, fringing, or sequined gowns, SS17 confirmed that stripes can be worn in any way.”

Wear: Embrace new textures and colours beyond cotton or standard navy and cream Breton stripes. There’s a rainbow line-up out there and the peekaboo effect of certain styles radiates sensuality.

MARCO DE VINCENZO

THE TRENCH COAT 3.0

SHOP NOW >

What: The timeless, traditional trench will always have a place in our heart and wardrobes, but the latest styles assume trophy status with bold new proportions and fabrications. Just tie them at the waist (the new erogenous zone) and go.

Who: Sonia Rykiel, A.W.A.K.E, Alessandra Rich, Isa Arfen, Y/Project, FLOW the Label.

Stephen says: “A must-have addition to any SS17 look, if you’re not ready to say goodbye to your layers or coats, this is the perfect compromise. From A.W.A.K.E’s multi textured and detailed take on a trans-seasonal trench to Sonia Rykiel’s khaki number we have it all.”

Wear: Keep warm all year round as they’re light enough to layer over knits now and bare skin come summer without missing a sartorial beat. Whether classic or more contemporary, the trench is a hardworking wardrobe hero with an irresistible tomboyish allure.

SHIRTS DECONSTRUCTED

SHOP NOW >

What: The button-up blouse proved to be ripe for reinvention and even though it was tweaked and twisted beyond recognition, it still elegantly ruled the runways. Knots, frills and cut-outs are some of the new striking details.

Who: Rosetta Getty, Isa Arfen, Com-un, 3.1 Phillip Lim, Victoria Victoria Beckham, Vivetta, Dodo Bar Or, Isabel Marant Étoile, Marco de Vincenzo, Vilshenko, Vivetta, Alessandra Rich, Alexander Wang, Clu, Haider Ackermann, Isa Arfen, Jonathan Simkhai, Sea NY, Shrimps.

Stephen says: “We saw the best of shirting this season with oversized and sexy one-shouldered styles thrown in too. The perfect way to spice up your work wardrobe, Rosetta Getty’s designs stole our hearts.”

Wear: Swapping your normal shirt for a new creative version is the easiest new season statement you can make. But don’t just save them for to work as they’ll give any look an intriguing ‘smart casual’ twist.

THE SHOULDER SHOW

SHOP NOW >

What: Experimentation with necklines, straps and sleeves has been rampant and as a result, shoulder-exposing necklines are more tantalising than ever before. From off-the-shoulder to strapless, clean or decorative, it’s simply a question of taste and temperature.

Who: Rosetta Getty, Beaufille, FLOW the label, Victoria by Victoria Beckham, All Things Mochi, Alice McCall, Gül Hürgel, Lisa Marie Fernandez, Maticevski, Preen by Thornton Bregazzi, Alice Temperley, Zimmermann, Rachel Comey, Antonio Berardi, Emilio de la Morena, Isa Arfen, Com-un, Jonathan Simkhai, Natasha Zinko, Sea NY, Sonia Rykiel, Ulla Johnson, Lemlem, Haider Ackermann.

Stephen says: “The exposed shoulder isn’t going anywhere fast and we saw it as another massive trend for SS17. But it does require adjustment and Beaufille have done just that. This silhouette is the ideal day to night weekend top, and a great way to show of a future summer tan.”

Wear: A polo neck underneath really ramps up the cost per wear ratio by ensuring these styles can be worn in the coldest climes. Then when the sun hits, they’ll frame shoulders and décolletages beautifully.

ROSETTA GETTY
Off The Shoulder Scarf Top and Skirt
 

RUFFLES REINVENTED

SHOP NOW >

What: Much cooler and sexier than before, the romantic ruffle appears as trims and tiers in unexpected fabrics and assorted dimensions for SS17. The ra-ra skirt references seen are a nod to the overriding ‘80s mood.

Who: 3.1 Phillip Lim, Marco de Vincenzo, Beaufille, Isabel Marant Étoile, Y/Project, Galvan, Gül Hürgel, Isa Arfen, Peter Pilotto, Rachel Comey, Antonio Berardi, Jonathan Simkhai, Paskal, Preen by Thornton Bregazzi, Rochas, Ganni, A.W.A.K.E, FLOW the Label, Lemlem.

Stephen says: “Whether they are big, small, neat or wild, on the arm or down the leg- ruffles have made a massive impact this season! Embrace a more playful, feminine way of dressing with ruffles being the main feature.”

Wear: Streamline the rest of your look so these fluid, sculptural features can be admired in all their glory. One style is more than enough to make a statement.

MICRO FLORALS

SHOP NOW >

What: We know how pretty and wedding-worthy florals are come spring, only this time they’re microscopic. You’ll find them strewn across dynamite dresses and sharp outerwear in a gorgeous riot of colour.

Who: Preen by Thornton Bregazzi, Marco de Vincenzo, Ulla Johnson, Needle & Thread, Perseverance, 3.1 Phillip Lim, Alexander Wang, FLOW the label, Leur Logette, Natasha Zinko, Saks Potts, Ulla Johnson, Vilshenko, Vivetta.

Stephen says: “This season’s florals may have been scaled downed and refined but they still command attention. Designers have given their creations a fresh gardenlike aura by covering them in tiny prints and motifs.”

Wear: Embrace the femininity by keep colours complimentary and accompanying accessories ladylike or go down the cool-girl route by adding boyish boots and a leather jacket. It’s a win-win.

THREE NEW MUST-BUY BRANDS

There’s a whole slew of emerging luxury brands poised to really breakthrough this season and we’re backing these three big time. Keep an eye out for their launches on Avenue 32.

 

GANNI

Spotted on all our favourite Instagram ‘it’ girls, Pandora Sykes and Camille Charriere, Ganni is a Danish label directed by Ditte Reffstrup that offers flirty day-to-day staples peppered with quirky cool details.

ANNIE COSTELLO BROWN

Statement earrings are trending and Los Angeles based jeweller Annie Costello Brown is making big waves with her geometric, warrior-like styles. Consider them as wearable works of art to reflect their meticulous handcraftsmanship.

BEAUFILLE

Beaufille, the brainchild of Canadian sisters Chloé and Parris Gordon, blends masculine and feminine codes artfully and intelligently. Right down to the name which translates as ‘handsome girl’. Fashion editors love it.
