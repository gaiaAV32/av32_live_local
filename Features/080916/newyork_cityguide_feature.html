FASHION WEEK CITY GUIDE: NEW YORK

 
Kicking off fashion week in the Big Apple? We’ve got just the list for you. Finding a decent latte and a place to lay your head after a long day may seem superfluous when you review your busy schedule but, in order to keep the reviews/news/Instagram-shots coming from dawn until dusk, utterly essential. And if you’ve not got a New York trip scheduled these hot spots are the perfect excuse to book a flight. Consider this your chicest city survival guide.
 

For a nightcap

The Twenty

 
Owned by New Zealander Jacob Willis, this bar is the latest to draw the city’s chic set to Williamsburg. Relaxed and unpretentious, it’s a great spot for late-night bites – devilled eggs, popcorn, grilled cheese – and killer cocktails
 

www.thetwentybar.com

For forty winks

New York Edition

 
Just a year old, the elegant New York Edition is already a favourite of fashion editors, developed by Ian Schrager (still synonymous with Studio 54). Respite from the punishing show schedule awaits in the hotel’s small spa, or for weary Brits, a plate of ‘chips’, not ‘fries’ in the Clocktower restaurant.
 

www.editionhotels.com

 

For carbohydrates

Pietro Nolita

 
Warning, it’s very pink. Heralded as a ‘healthy Italian’, the restaurant is inspired by the cooking of cofounder Pietro Quaglia’s Milanese mother, housed in a rose-tinted Jeanette Dalrot designed space. Fun fact: Quaglia was fashion editor and street-style cat nip Giovanna Battaglia’s best man at her recent Capri wedding, so expect the fash pack in full force.
 

www.pietronolita.com

For people watching

Kola House

 
Opening during fashion week, Kola House is a new venture from Pepsi – yes, the drinks company. With everything centred on the kola nut, the bitter fruit containing caffeine that gives cola beverages their name, expect experimental dishes and a lot of Instagram-buzz.
 

www.kolahouse.com

 

For a caffeine fix

The Bowery Market

 
Opening earlier this year, The Bowery Market is essentially a boutique food hall. Home to five vendors, the space is set up as an outdoor market. Our go-to? The Butcher’s Daughter kiosk, for juices and superior avo-toast, and Champion Coffee for exactly what it says on the tin.
 

www.thebowerymarket.com

For brunch

The Wild Son

 
A healthy affair, this brunch spot in the Meatpacking District features the familiar pancakes and breakfast sandwiches but with a more virtuous twist. Don’t miss the ‘Doughnut of the Day’ – there’s even a vegan option.
 

www.thewildsonnyc.com

 

For a culture fix

Cornelia Parker at The Met Fifth Avenue

 
Head to the roof of The Met to catch British artist Cornelia Parker’s large scale sculpture Transitional Object (PsychoBarn). Inspired by the paintings of Edward Hopper, the classic American red barn, and the Bates family’s sinister mansion from Alfred Hitchcock’s Psycho, the structure is an unusual contrast to Manhattan’s famous skyline.
 

www.metmuseum.org

For matcha

Cha Cha Matcha

 
Founded by son of Hard Rock Café cofounder Peter Morton and a former NYC classmate after a trip to Uji, Japan, this is the place Gigi Hadid and Taylor Hill head to for coconut matcha lattes, matcha pastries, matcha soft-serve… Anything matcha, ok.
 

www.chachamatcha.com
