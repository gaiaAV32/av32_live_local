NEW YORK FASHION WEEK

 

Bright lights, big city, huge design talent

 
With New York Fashion Week the starting pistol is fired for five weeks of non-stop fashion shows – and while that schedule might be exhausting, there’s nothing hard work about these designers who make head-turning style look so easy.
 

Rosetta GETTY

SHOP NOW >
 

1

The namesake brand Rosetta Getty launched in 2014 wasn’t her first fashion foray. She began modelling aged 14 and previously had a successful childrenswear line.

2

With director Chantal Akerman as her muse for AW16, Getty wrapped, tied and layered polished pieces revealing flashes of skin beneath exquisite fabrics.

3

Getty has Hollywood pedigree. Los Angeles based and married to actor Balthazar Getty, she’s now the go-brand for stylish starlets seeking cool, clean and captivating elegance.

4

Girls of the moment Alicia Vikander and Margot Robbie have both worn the collection; close friend Patricia Arquette wore Getty to scoop her Academy Award in 2015.

5

Very clean, minimal and classic – but interesting and eccentric at the same time” is how Getty described her aesthetic to us. We couldn’t agree more.

6

Eschewing runway shows altogether, Getty puts her collections into context with striking photography shot by Charlie Engman and a series of ultra-chic events like her annual Tuscan summer party.
 
 

Jonathan SIMKHAI

SHOP NOW >
 

1

The native New Yorker and 2015 CFDA/Vogue Award winner is following in the footsteps of his grandfather who owned a lace and embroidery factory in Iran.

2

Simkhai started young – as a 14 year old buying apprentice at Habana Jeans. His first designs were improvised beach-to-bar cover-ups made for friends from his own shirts.

3

Simkhai’s incredible flair for fabrics and silhouettes is his USP. Manipulating menswear codes with fearless femininity and athletic accents, his sexy styles span day and night.

4

“I definitely design for powerful women,” Simkhai told us. “I want them to look in the mirror and feel they can take on the world”.

5

Balancing wearability with wow factor, Simkhai’s youthful designs also radiate New York’s urban confidence, appealing to the likes of Blake Lively and Emily Ratajkowski.

6

Inspired by architect Santiago Calatrava’s suspended structures, for AW16 Simkhai gives us powerful pieces defined by futuristic lace, zig-zagging hems and inky colours.
 
 

3.1 Phillip LIM

SHOP NOW >
 

1

Celebrating 10 years in business last year, Phillip Lim pioneered the contemporary fashion category with his collections of stylish staples with a twist.

2

“I want to make clothes I desire and clothes that make my customer feel beautiful and confident. It’s really simple,” Lim told Vogue.com.

3

The effortlessness and generation-defying charm of Lim’s urban boy-meets-girl aesthetic is reflected by fans as diverse as Kiernan Shipka, Alexa Chung and Michelle Obama.

4

Reimagining the Japanese kimono with a utilitarian approach for AW16, tomboyish cuts and flirty fits also gain lavish new textures and inventive embellishment.

5

Standout accessories like the Pashli satchel, have the same edgy yet effortless appeal as the ready-to-wear collections. No wonder they’re streetstyle fodder.

6

The fashion world can’t get enough of Lim; he scooped the 2007 CFDA Swarovski Award for Womenswear and the CFDA Accessories Designer of the Year gong in 2013.
 
 

Alexander WANG

SHOP NOW >
 

1

In 2004, the then 21-year old Alexander Wang dropped out of Parsons to launch his namesake line. The risk was worth it; today the designer is one of the biggest forces in contemporary fashion.

2

Arguably the author of that model-off-duty look, Wang’s creations are defined by their progressive, streetwise attitude and ultra-cool mix of grunge, athleticism and just the right amount of polish.

3

The hottest NYFW ticket and a legendary after-party host, Wang’s gaggle of friends and muses include some of the coolest girls in the world: Erin Wasson, Zoe Kravitz and Dree Hemingway.
 

Margot Robbie

Alexander Wang

4

After a meteoric fashion rise and three year stint as Balenciaga’s creative director, Wang is now focusing on his own expansive brand: womenswear, menswear, diffusion line T by Wang and accessories.

5

“I like to think of my girl as someone who really has a less precious way of approaching fashion. She enjoys it, she loves it, but she really takes things and places them out of context,” Wang told WWD.com.

6

For AW16, Wang subverts the glamour of ladylike shapes with ’80s and ’90s club kid references. Expect to see the bleach splattered prints, lace and biker chain inserts, and irreverent slogans on the hippest girls around.
