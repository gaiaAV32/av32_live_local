FANTASY TO REALITY: NEW YORK ICONS

 

Be inspired by the city’s big screen style queens

 
Dream dinner dates can move over. Imagine if, instead, you were asked to pick three quintessential New York icons whose wardrobes (and lives) have you’d like to get your hands on? A much better game, we think you’ll agree. And so, as New York Fashion Week kicks off a new season and we lie in wait for trends and star pieces beyond our wildest daydreams, we hark back to the city’s finest TV and film heroines – what better inspiration for season-transcending style?
 
 

The chic muse:

Holly Golightly

 
Audrey Hepburn’s star turn in Breakfast at Tiffany’s saw her style icon status cemented – her character’s ensembles standing the test of time. Think pink, plus oversized shirts, elegant black gowns and kissing in the rain in a trench-coat.
 
 

The experimental muse:

Carrie Bradshaw

 
Patricia Field’s costumes for Sex and the City oracle Carrie were plentiful, but a penchant for a knee length full skirt, quirky clutch and sensational stilettos were glorious constants (not to mention way more desirable than Big et al).
 
 

The androgynous muse:

Annie Hall

 
Woody Allen’s tomboyish Annie Hall has been cited as inspiration for many an outfit by Alexa Chung and Elizabeth Olsen. From oversized jackets, to loose-fit trousers and buttoned-up shirts, just borrow your boyfriend’s tie to finish things off.
