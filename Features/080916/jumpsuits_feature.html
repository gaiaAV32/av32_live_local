JUMPSUITS: A WEARER’S GUIDE

 

The do’s and don’ts of the wardrobe game changer

 
Ah, the jumpsuit. The ’70s staple has danced its way from the disco into every woman’s wardrobe. Packing a oneand- done punch, the sexy simplicity of this ensemble has made fans of Françoise Hardy (in iridescent chain mail, or astride a motorbike), Sienna Miller (in clean white for Wimbledon) and Kendall Jenner (let us count the ways). The cleverness is in the cut of the top, meaning there’s one to suit every occasion and every body type from shoulderbearing to big-sleeved, high-necked or featuring a deep breastbone-revealing V-neck. Somehow along the way a myth emerged that they’re tricky to pull off, but as always, we’re here to help. Behold, your guide to this gamechanging garment.

DO

 

1.

Get up, pull it on and go – no faffing about whether tops and bottoms go together.

2.

Feel free to turn cartwheels in the park on your lunch hour.

3.

Substitute for your old faithful LBD – just as classic, just as flattering, no need for tights. Some styles even have half-corsets sewn in for support and good posture.

4.

Consider for your rehearsal dinner/engagement drinks/weddings as a more modern she-can-wear-the-trousers-too statement (see Audrey Gelman’s Detroit nuptials for a masterclass).

5.

Get it tailored. Labels like Galvan make their jumpsuits’ legs long, to hem for any height, and leave extra seam allowance in the crotch for those of long torso.
 
 

DON'T

 

1.

Layer with a leotard. We’ve all suffered as the victim of a small bathroom stall.

2.

Forget jewellery – with a simple black jumpsuit as your foundation, there’s plenty of fun to be had with accessories, namely a great big neck nuzzling statement earring.

3.

Pack away at the end of the season. Wear all year round – solo in the summer with sandals, and with stacked heels and a shirt or polo neck underneath come winter.

4.

Wear frilly knickers. There’s a time and place, and a VPL is no one’s friend. Seamless is the only way to go.

5.

Underestimate the joy of pockets. Ulla Johnson’s has them, as does Rachel Comey’s, and Leur Logette. Just don’t overstuff and ruin the hallowed ‘line’.
