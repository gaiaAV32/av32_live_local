A FRESH PERSPEX-TIVE

 

The making of Lily Kamper’s geometric gems.

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Who says knockout jewellery has to be metallic or studded with stones? Not us and not Lily Kamper, the London based jeweller who crafts the elegant, pillar-like pendants from plastic (yes, you read that right). Her alchemising of the ordinary material in extraordinary, sculptural collections is a painstaking process: blocks of Perspex and resin are hand-turned on a lathe (imagine a potter’s wheel) then hand-dyed. Look a little closer and you’ll notice how beautifully expressive the colours Kamper mixes, again by hand, are; and how decoratively step-like ridges, which reference Art Deco architecture, American artist Dan Flavin and more, disrupt the symmetrical pendants’ smooth surfaces.
