WHO is Arthur Arbesser?

Born in Vienna, Arthur Arbesser studied at Central Saint Martins before moving to Milan to work under Giorgio Armani. In 2013, he struck out on his own and founded his eponymous brand. His first presentation was shown in the intimacy of a friend’s apartment – though that didn’t stop the international fashion press from knocking. In 2015 Arbesser was an LVMH prize finalist and is credited with being one of the young guns shaking up Milan Fashion Week. And what about his woman? “Sophisticated, very sharp and she loves to feel special in an understated way,” the designer explains.

WHAT?

The superior tailoring skills Arbesser picked up during his time at Armani show in the precise, exquisitely-executed cuts of his designs. His clean lined silhouettes are then ignited by rich colours, luxe fabrics and vivid prints. As the man himself puts it, “I feel today more than ever we need character and a very personal point of view to stand out. That’s how I see the Arthur Arbesser aesthetic: different but classy, unique and effortless”. Certainly the label has lashings of character, drawing on influences from the different cities he’s lived in. “Growing up in Vienna made me realize how precious the past is; Viennese architecture and design of Vienna at the turn of last century keeps being a strong inspiration on my work,” he says. “But it was the four years studying at St.Martins really opened my eyes, made me realise that clashing the old wit the edgy makes the message stronger. Now Milan with its hidden charms and strong design history is becoming equally important to me... I just love to tell stories through clothes and all these cities have a lot to tell”.

WHY do you need it now?

In a word: velvet. Arbesser was the force behind some of AW16’s most desirable and fresh iterations of the season-defining fabric, investing it with an unexpected lightness. Take that buttercup floor-length dress, for instance, in Arbesser’s deft hands it becomes fluid, easy, simple. In a collection inspired by “a mix of Vienna around 1900, the thoughtful work of Belgian artist Michael Borremans and Flemish floral paintings of the 17th century” you’ll also find thick corduroy in jewel tones and a leaf print created in collaboration with French illustrator Agathe Singer.
 
Arthur Arbesser’s AW16 moodboard. Image via @arthurarbesser
 

WHERE next for the brand?

Arbesser’s collections resonates with the kind of creative women who appreciate good design, and like to invest in a forever-item rather than a flash in the pan trend, so expect to see the AW16 pieces at this year’s art fair circuit and beyond. Arbesser’s star is rising fast; in 2015 he was made creative director of Iceberg. For now, the globetrotter is happy to be carving out his niche where he is. “Milan is having a very good moment right now,” Arbesser says. “The city has started to realise that it’s not just the great ‘Made in Italy’ craftsmanship and perfect Italian fabrics which count, but also a fresh eye and energy”. A fresh eye and energy – we could say the same about him.
