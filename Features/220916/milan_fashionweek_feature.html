MILAN FASHION WEEK

 

Who’s who in the Italian renaissance.

 
Discover the designers redefining modern femininity and shaking up the Milan show schedule, one scene-stealing piece at a time.
 

Arthur ARBESSER

COMING SOON 
 

1

A Central Saint Martins graduate who decided not to set up shop in London, Arbesser launched his eponymous brand in 2013. In 2015 he was appointed Creative Director of Iceberg.

2

Balancing originality with versatility via dynamic designs and expressive fabric choices, the designer won a Who Is On Next? award and a spot on the 2015 LVMH prize shortlist.

3

Tailoring is Arbesser’s forte, reflecting seven years spent at Italian powerhouse Armani. Agathe Singer’s vivid watercolours adorned his exceptional cuts this season and last.

Arthur Arbesser

4

High-necked and low hemmed; the Austrian brightens up the fluid lines of his AW16 collection with yellow velvet and cornflower blue corduroy. That’s ’70s style evening and outerwear sorted then.

5

“The AW16 collection has a sophisticated feel. The inspiration comes from turn of the century Vienna and the contemporary Belgian painter Michaël Borremans,” Arbesser told us.

6

Regular visits to the Viennese opera sparked the selfconfessed nerd’s fascination with fashion and costume. He also references contemporary art and architecture, shown by his progressive presentations.
 
 

Marco DE VINCENZO

SHOP NOW >
 

1

Italian Vogue Editor-in-chief Franca Sozzani loved de Vincenzo’s debut haute couture collection so much she awarded him her magazine’s prestigious Who Is On Next? award in 2009.

2

The former Fendi designer blurs the boundaries between good and bad taste: clashing directional details, lavish textures and vibrant colours to glorious and glamourous effect.

3

Only de Vincenzo’s artisanal craftsmanship and streamlined silhouettes can be predicted. AW16’s maximalist masterclass is refined and decorated with asymmetric ruffles, diagonal pleats and chilli prints.

Marco De Vincenzo
 

4

Famed for his flamboyant footwear; the designer’s braided heels, satin woven flats and velvet-bowed boots (balanced on sculptural heels) assume hero status while elegantly transcending trends.

5

“I believe that my creations are an interesting cross between minimalism and decorative art. Shapes and proportions must never be exaggerated or distort a woman’s body,” de Vincenzo told vogue.it.

6

Follow fans Giovanna Engelbert (nee Battaglia), Anna Dello Russo and Sofie Valkiers’ lead as their streetstyle shots offer ample styling tips for de Vincenzo’s designers. Beyoncé is also partial to his fearless creations.
 
 

Philosophy DI LORENZO SERAFINI

SHOP NOW >
 

1

A giant of Italian fashion, Alberta Ferretti founded her diffusion line Philosophy in 1984; former Dolce & Gabbana and Roberto Cavalli designer Lorenzo Serafini took the reins in 2014.

2

Synonymous with soft sensuality, Serafini adds a modern edge to Philosophy’s retro-romantic roots, attracting the likes of Alexa Chung, Alessandra Ambrosio and Suki Waterhouse as fans in the proces

3

The ultra-feminine signatures include cascading ruffles, intricate lace, Liberty-esque florals, pastel tones and diaphanous silhouettes. In short, pieces that make you feel like the heroine of a romantic novel.

4

An collector of vintage books and magazines, the Italian also draws inspiration from muses Brooke Shields in Pretty Baby and Chloë Sevigny, and ’90’s Ferretti campaigns shot by Steven Meisel.

5

Dreamy dresses and trophy separates made in the spirit of ‘80s icons Princess Diana and Siouxsie Sioux characterise the AW16 collection. Translucent textures and plunging necklines ramp up desirability.

6

“A spontaneous woman with an effortless attitude and a sensual mind,” is how the designer described the Philosophy di Lorenzo Serafini woman to vogue.fr.
 
 

VIVETTA

SHOP NOW >
 

1

Designer Vivi Ponti mastered embroidery at Roberto Cavalli before launching her quirky namesake label Vivetta in 2008. Everything is designed and made in Italy using the finest fabrics.

2

Revamping prim shapes with surreal motifs, sweet but punchy palettes and imaginative embroidery; the Italian’s girlish designs prove subtly provocative and are worn by Alexa Chung and Maisie Williams.

3

Along with those signature (not to mention Insta-fabulous) hand motif collars, other whimsical, outfit-upgrading hallmarks include detachable bows, silhouette portraits and dainty ruffles.

Vivi Ponti

4

Ponti delivers a delightful selection of ’70s inspired shapes for AW16. Besides the shots of pillarbox red and ethereal flounces, there are elaborate trompe-l’oeil prints and bejeweled buttons to play with.

5

“In this collection, Erté’s Art Deco aesthetic meets with my surrealist style, creating playful pieces and anthropomorphic detailing: graceful female silhouettes and striking blue-eyed faces,” Ponti told us.

6

Ponti’s collections usually contain animals and her favourite colour, blue. A lover of Lula magazine, the designer’s enlisted founder Leith Clark to style Vivetta’s fashion shows and lookbooks.
