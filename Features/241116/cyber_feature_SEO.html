FOUNDER ROBERTA BENTELER

 

1

HAIDER ACKERMANN
Monochrome Mohair Chevron Stripe Dress
 
WHO? The man behind actress Tilda Swinton’s most admired red carpet looks; Haider Ackermann launched his eponymous label in Paris in 2001. Since then, the Columbian fashion designer has garnered a cult following for his rock ‘n’ roll tailoring, sensual drapery and captivating colour palettes.
WHAT NOW? Ackermann was a leading force in Autumn Winter 2016’s velvet trend and also thrust mohair into the spotlight via this chevron stripe maxi. This sophisticated dress is as perfectly cut as his incredible trousers.
ROBERTA SAYS: An effortless evening style that proves how ageless and flattering chevron stripes are.
WEAR TO... Make an entrance at work, wedding, whenever really with understated accessories. Additional colour is optional.

2

PASKAL
Blush 3-D Flower Embellished Coat EXCLUSIVE
 
WHO? Flying the flag for Ukrainian fashion design in the west, Julie Paskal subverts her feminine ready-to-wear designer clothing with laser cutting and architectural proportions. Her designs are Vogue endorsed and shown as part of Paris Fashion Week.
WHAT NOW? This mannish coat, an Avenue 32 exclusive, illustrates many of Paskal’s signatures: clean lines, pretty but progressive use of pink, impactful 3D flower arrangements and a statement-making versatility that endures regardless.
ROBERTA SAYS: Winter pastels are gaining traction and Paskal’s boy-meets-girl coat is a cool way to embrace them.
WEAR TO... Lighten up a dark winter wardrobe and sharpen floaty frocks. Consider blush a neutral beside tan, navy and olive.

3

NO.21
Grigio Snake Diamante Heel Boots
 
WHO? No.21’s timeless, trophy footwear is the brainchild of Alessandro Dell’Acqua. Reflecting the Italian fashion designer’s artistry and dedication to couture-like craftsmanship, No.21 shoes star in countless streetstyle star albums each season.
WHAT NOW? Following in the footsteps of the mule and sneaker, it’s the failsafe ankle boot’s turn to get the No.21 treatment. Cue origami bows and glittering heels that push these otherwise classic snakeskin boots into outfit-transformer territory.
ROBERTA SAYS: If heels hurt but you’d still like a little height and sparkle, these bold boots are the answer.
WEAR TO... Boost your staple looks- jeans and jumpers, midis and t-shirts, even LBDs and expect compliments with every step.
 
 

FASHION DIRECTOR STEPHEN AYRES

 

1

MIRA MIKATI
Green Eyelet Smile Coat
 
WHO? Beyoncé backed Mira Mikati early on by wearing one of her cartoon coats. Scattering colourful emoji and witty slogans across ready-to-wear and sneakers, the Lebanese designer cheers up wardrobes and lifts moods with her delightful designs.
WHAT NOW? This warm, double-breasted coat is the only one you’ll need this winter as it works hard and plays hard 24/7. We dare you not to ‘smile’ when you discover the word spelled out in colourful eyelets of the back.
STEPHEN SAYS: A fun way to buck the ‘black coat’ trend and inject personality into your outerwear.
WEAR TO... Animate your winter layers. Business at the front, party at the back, you’ll stand out in an inevitable sea of plain coats.

2

SEA NY
Black Combo Confetti Sleeveless Top
 
WHO? Monica Paolini and Sean Monahan’s ready-to-wear brand Sea NY blends uptown polish with downtown attitude in the Big Apple. Expect easy day-to night heroes that boast charming boho touches and exquisite embroidery.
WHAT NOW? In short: trans-seasonal trophy tops. Colourful eyelash appliqué sets this boxy style playfully apart. It looks even better in real life and up close so the texture and technique can be admired in all their glory.
STEPHEN SAYS: Even black as decorative as this goes with almost everything.
WEAR TO... A centrepiece in its own right and the lead layer you’ve been waiting for: wear over shirts and dresses.

3

BUILDING BLOCK
Navy Leather Tassel Bucket Bag EXCLUSIVE
 
WHO? Building Block founders and sisters Kimberly and Nancy Wu create sophisticated designer bags using simplified shapes, wooden accents and metallic hardware. Cylinders become roomy duffles, for instance, while front flap shoulder bags are rectangular and there’s a square, top-handle style.
WHAT NOW? Everyday minimalism mastered, this rich navy bag is an Avenue 32 exclusive and a brilliant addition to any collection. The drawstring closure means it’s bigger than it looks so you can carry everything you need.
STEPHEN SAYS: The goes-with-everything tone, medium size and edgy closure ensures this bag will never go out of style.
WEAR TO... Stylishly upgrade your daily uniform. Shoulder it or wear cross body depending on your outfit/mood.
 
 

BUYING MANAGER LEEANNE WALSH

 

1

JONATHAN SIMKHAI
Navy Fringed Culottes
 
WHO? A master of sexy-sporty chic and wonderful fabric manipulation, Jonathan Simkhai won the CFDA/Vogue Fashion Fund in 2015. The New Yorker’s head-turning cocktail dresses and tailoring are designed to empower women and are worn by Cate Blanchett and Gigi Hadid.
WHAT NOW? When Simkhai dabbles in daywear, he revamps essential separates with the marvellous and polished details he’s known for. Enter these fringe trim culottes, cut with a flattering kick flare, that add a graceful swish with every step.
LEEANNE SAYS: The waist-whittling, body-skimming shape will covert anyone still unsure about culottes.
WEAR TO... A great alternative to the cocktail dress that can also be worn to work- tuck in any top.

2

ROKSANDA
Multi Silk Panel Emmerich Dress
 
WHO? Roksanda is well-versed in creating elegant yet experimental pieces that meet the sartorial demands of modern women around the clock. The award-winning designer is a London Fashion Week regular who dresses FLOTUS Michelle Obama.
WHAT NOW? Inspired by The Night Porter film and Deborah Turbeville’s photography, this languid silk dress epitomises the darker, poetic romance of Roksanda’s AW16 collection with signature colour-blocking. Who knew deep purple, teal and copper went together so beautifully?
LEEANNE SAYS: Demure but dramatic: this is an effortless occasion dress.
WEAR TO... Any event or party where you don’t want to look too try hard. Add ladylike shoes and go.

3

VILSHENKO
Black Silk Embroidered Lucy Blouse
 
WHO? A firm favourite of Alexa Chung and Man Repeller Leandra Medine, Olga Vilshenko’s romantic silhouettes showcase the spectacular folkloric motifs she creates for every collection. From easy blouses to entrance-making gowns and capes, this is boho patterned to perfection.
WHAT NOW? Black has been used to balance Vilshenko’s vivid embellishments this season so the intricate prints and embroidery make an even bigger impact. The impressive mosaic print of this blouse transforms it from ordinary to extraordinary.
LEEANNE SAYS: A ‘I just threw this on and I look great’ blouse for all shapes and sizes.
WEAR TO... Accompany your favourite trousers- it will dress up denim, dress down tailoring and expertly temper leather.
