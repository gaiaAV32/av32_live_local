PARIS FASHION WEEK

Paris, fashion month’s finale, never disappoints because the upcoming season’s trends and talking points are confirmed. Established and emerging stars shine bright and despite their differences, our luxury French favourites inspire the same fevered following and enduring loyalty.

 

 

 
 
 

Haider Ackermann

Chances are you’ve already admired Haider Ackermann’s avant garde designs as the Columbian’s friend and frequent collaborator actress Tilda Swinton is usually wearing him on the red carpet. Industry insiders also turn to the talented draper and renowned colour master for wear-forever trousers, androgynous tailoring and striking, sensual silhouettes conjured from breathtaking fabrics.

Sonia Rykiel

The late Sonia Rykiel’s legacy of free-spirted, luxury clothes doused in irreverent Left-Bank glamour lives on under current Creative Director Julie de Libran. Beyond the hit knits and ‘70s style silhouettes, there are whimsical and infinitely collectable accessories- knockout bags, cool-girl shoes, straw hats and more to complete the bewitching vision head to toe.

Y Project

Part of Paris’s exciting new guard and the brains behind Rihanna’s Anti World Tour costumes, Glenn Martens ensures his experimental creations reflect the diversity of modern society with menswear and womenswear elements. Focused on form, pieces like the signature souped-up sweatshirt tend to have a streetwise slant and inventive details that make them adjustable.
