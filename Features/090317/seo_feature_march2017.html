AVENUE 32’S FASHION MONTH ROUND-UP

 
The saying ‘It’s not what you wear but the way that you wear it’ has never rung truer this Fashion Month. Against a background of ongoing shows of designer clothes and changeable weather, the street style set pulled off the SS17 trends with commendable flair. Look to them for instant inspiration- our very own Roberta Benteler was left and centre- as it’s so easy to recreate their looks and try out their styling tricks using our latest luxury designer fashion arrivals. We’ve broken down five of them down below, with everything else you need to know, in a helpful round-up that you can reference for seasons to come. The overarching mood? ‘Timeless with a twist’ as the coveted women’s designer clothing seen nodded to the trends without compromising their impressive cost-per-wear ratio. Read, learn, shop and we’ll ship your resulting purchases worldwide.
 
 
 
 
 

TREND:

TRENCH COATS

What: Anyone who was anyone swapped their puffas and fun faux fur for the trench coat’s latest guises then basked in their reflected glory. Whether in beige but updated with new proportions, panels and straps or ladylike plastic; on the shoulder or off; belted in or left loose- they proved to be ideal over cold-busting layers with peeks of contrasting sleeves and hoods poking out the back.
 
Spotted on: Avenue 32 founder Roberta Benteler, British Vogue’s Sarah Harris, We The People’s Jessie Bush, Stylist Natasha Goldenberg
 
The Go-to Brands: Sonia Rykiel, A.W.A.K.E, Alessandra Rich, Isa Arfen, Y/Project, Roksanda, FLOW the Label

TREND: SHIRTING

What: Shirt wearers were just as experimentally elegant as the styles themselves. Upgraded with voluminous sleeves, cut-outs, knots and much more, they were most commonly tucked into jeans to make the ultimate boy-meets-girl statement. Even if a classic button-up was preferred, the colour wasn’t and it was pulled down to expose a bare or knit covered shoulder, layered and sleeves were left trailing.
 
Spotted on: Bloggers Camille Charriere and Linda Tol, Avenue 32 Creative and Editorial Director Romilly Mason, fashion editor Giovanna Engelbert
 
The Go-to Brands: Rosetta Getty, Isa Arfen, Com-un, 3.1 Phillip Lim, Victoria Victoria Beckham, Vivetta, Dodo Bar Or, Isabel Marant Étoile, Marco de Vincenzo, Vilshenko,  Alessandra Rich, Alexander Wang

TREND: CHECKS

What: Checks, tartans, plaids, tweeds– whatever you’re calling them –were out in force in lieu of stripes as the go-to print. XS to XL, they were jam-packed with colour and personality and often paired with simple complementary separates. One discerning dresser even doubled up to eye-catching effect, while gingham emerged as a fabulous foil to leather. Vintage-looking checks were expertly revived with a throw-over-anything mentality.
 
Spotted on: Fashion editor Christine Centenera, creative consultant Kate Foley, fashion director Caroline Issa, stylist Rachael Wang
 
The Go-to Brands: Beaufille, Isa Arfen, Isabel Marant Étoile, Marco de Vincenzo, Shrimps, Sonia Rykiel, A.W.A.K.E, Saks Potts, Ace & Jig

TREND: PINK

What: Pink lover or not, you can’t deny it’s captivating femininity and the streets were awash with every hue from the lightest blush to deep fuchsia. Beginners dabbled in accessories, while others really revelled in the colour’s head-turning power by rocking it head to toe alone or with some neutrals and a pop of red thrown in for good measure. Any shape or spirit goes.

TREND: DENIM

What: Long gone are the days when ‘casual’ denim would have been left at home. We spied the favoured fabric everywhere- as flattering jeans in every wash and fit left plain, frayed or embellished; as day-to-night tops, trusty jackets, lightweight coats, pencil skirts etc. The question wasn’t if you were wearing it but how many pieces could be worn at once. Two, three, maybe even four for the really hard-core.
 
Spotted on: Chanel muse Caroline de Maigret, Stylist Alex Carl, blogger Irina Lakicevic, Miroslava Duma
 
The Go-to Brands: Sea NY, Victoria Victoria Beckham, Vivetta, Needle & Thread, Sonia Rykiel, Isabel Marant Étoile, Y/Project, Robert Clergerie, Beaufille, Rachel Comey
