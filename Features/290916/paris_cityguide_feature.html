FASHION WEEK CITY GUIDE: PARIS

 
Last but not least, the City of Lights. The fourth and final city of fashion month is the home of couture, filled with romance and laced with a certain je ne sais quoi. There are hundreds of worthy eateries and spots to hang out, but we’ve taken out the hard work of the search and rounded up a few of the finest for you. Enjoy.
 
 

For brunch

Soya

 
Paris eateries are notoriously tricky for vegetarians to navigate, but with an all-you-can-eat vegan brunch, Soya is at hand to maintain energy levels.
 
20 Rue de la Pierre Levée, 75011 Paris.
Phone: +33 1 48 06 33 02

For a coffee

The Broken Arm

 
Founded by a collective of style bloggers The Broken Arm is a concept storecum- café stocking design books and kitchenware alongside fresh juices, coffee and a lunch menu by Swedish chef Linda Granebring.
 
12 Rue Perrée, 75003 Paris.
Phone: +33 1 44 61 53 60
 
 

For a culture fix

Olafur Eliasson at Versailles

 
It’s your last chance to catch Versailles’ guest artist Olafur Eliasson’s striking installations before the exhibition ends on 20th October. Plan on sticking in the city? Head to the Gagosian Gallery’s Paris outpost for a show Duane Hanson’s realist human sculptures.
 
Château de Versailles, Place d’Armes - 78000 Versailles.
Phone : +33 (0)1 30 83 78 00.
Gagosian Gallery, 4 Rue de Ponthieu, 75008 Paris.

For flowers

Rambert Rigaud Fleuriste en Herbe

 
Who doesn’t love fresh flowers? Whether it’s for a big installation, a homesick assistant’s birthday or just because, Isabel Marant’s favourite florist will have just the thing. Ex-studio director at Yves Saint Laurent and Dior, Rigaud stocks exquisite orchids.
 
2 Rue de l’Université, 75007 Paris, France
 
 

For forty winks

L'Hotel

 
The final home of Oscar Wilde (demands for him to pay his hotel bill are framed on the walls) and 10 minutes’ walk from the Louvre, this boutique hotel on the Left Bank has an underground pool and a Michelin-starred restaurant. Be sure to book a table two or three weeks ahead, it’s that good.
 
13 Rue des Beaux Arts, 75006 Paris, France.
Phone: +33 1 44 41 99 00

For food

Ferdi

 
Named after the son of Ferdi’s owners Alicia and Jack Fontanier ‘because he’s our best creation’, menu favourites include a cheeseburger, ceviche, rich ratatouille and ice cream with chocolate sauce. Regulars include Azzedine Alaїa, Alber Elbaz and Marc Jacobs.
 
32 Rue du Mont Thabor, 75001 Paris.
Phone: +33 6 51 70 29 70
 
 

For dancing

Silencio

 
Named after the creepy theatre in Mulholland Drive, David Lynch’s Paris nightclub is six flights of stairs down, and has a reflective dance floor, cinema, library, and a dream forest of yellow steel trees. Fun fact: the gold leaf is applied by the same guys who touch up Napoleon’s tomb.
 
142 Rue Montmartre, 75002 Paris.
Phone: +33 1 40 13 12 33

For chocolate

Debauve et Gallais

 
Napoleon’s official chocolatier is still going strong and their marrons glacés and candied chestnuts are a favourite of designer Giambattista Valli. Take home as a treat for family and friends who held the fort while you were away.
 
33 Rue Vivienne, 75002 Paris.
Phone: +33 1 40 39 05 50
 
 

For yoga

Rasa Yoga

 
In the shadows of Notre Dame, this white-walled peaceful haven with bilingual teachers is a favourite with off-duty models. Squeeze in a vinyasa class between appointments and you’ll feel all the better for it.
 
21 Rue Saint-Jacques, 75005 Paris.
Phone: +33 1 43 54 14 59
