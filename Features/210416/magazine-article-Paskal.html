PASKAL PINK

 

Ukraine’s name to know

 

Blush Pink Laser-Cut Dress, Blush Pink Crepe Scaled Jacket and A-line Skirt

 
 
 

If you’re pondering pink this season but favour clothes that are punchy rather than prim, edge towards the grown-up over the girlie, and like an elegant aesthetic only when it comes with an experimental edge, then it’s time to get acquainted with Paskal’s SS16 collection. Scalpel sharp cuts, rows of layered and perforated circles and a smattering of neon origami pompoms create a selection of fresh and original occasionwear. The blush palette injects romance, softening the decorative minimalism and signature laser cuts that the Ukrainian brand is known for, while skin peeks playfully through adding another alluring feature.

Shape, structure and silhouette is vital to Julie Paskal’s aesthetic and this season the architecture graduate turned designer drew inspiration from the beaches of her hometown, Odessa, and the naked body, which is reflected in a collection that possesses a breezy charm beneath its formality. Part of the influx of East European fashion talent making waves globally, the mother of two launched her luxury brand in 2011 and it was shortlisted for the LVMH prize in 2014. With everything made in Ukraine and shown in Kiev and Paris, Paskal’s mainstream momentum is only getting stronger.
