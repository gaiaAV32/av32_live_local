Passion, determination and an impeccable knowledge of the industry are the ingredients Julia Schygulla says you need to make it in fashion. And she should know. Starting at German InStyle as an intern Schygulla rose rapidly up the ranks – and masthead – to be appointed fashion news director in 2014, a role that has seen her interview everyone from Carine Roitfeld to Karl Lagerfeld (for the record: “both kind and humble, I will never, ever delete the interviews from my dictaphone”). She sat down to talk to Avenue 32 about new season trends, forever pieces and her style icons...

‘I love interesting shapes and materials, but I’m not that into bright colours and prints. My wardrobe is mostly monochrome...’

 
 

On her personal style...
My personal style is more classic then trend driven. I love interesting shapes and materials, but I’m not that into bright colours and prints. My wardrobe is mostly monochrome with additional hues of navy, light blue, grey and burgundy.

On the pieces every woman should own...
A white shirt, whether it’s a cotton oxford or a silk blouse. Plus other classics: a black cashmere turtleneck, a perfectly fitting pair of jeans, a sharp blazer and a little black dress in the shape that fits your body type best. Except for a great pair of shoes and a bag, my ultimate investment piece is a coat. And everyone needs a piece of jewellery given to them by someone they love.

On her dream wardrobe raids
Past: Ali MacGraw. Present: Vanessa Traina. Movie: Cate Blanchett in Blue Jasmine. On

On the SS16 trends she’s most excited to try...
I collect blouses; my closet is full of them. This season they come in more shapes and with more details then ever before. So I’ll definitely add some more to my collection, like a playful one from Vivetta or one of the elegant blouses by Macgraw. I wear lots of jeans and I’m very much into flares at the moment. A pair of cropped ones is definitely on my shopping list for spring.

On the Avenue names she’s most excited about...
Through Avenue 32 I discovered the Australian brand Macgraw. I could order everything from the collection; it’s feminine yet cool, classic yet modern. My favourite style is the apron dress paired with a lace blouse underneath. I also love Isa Arfen’s silhouettes and shapes and Vivetta’s fun collection is great for adding a little quirkiness to your wardrobe. Vilshenko dresses already have me dreaming of summer nights. And if there’s an evening event I’ll pick a Galvan dress or jumpsuit for sure; their tailoring is impeccable and they manage to do the impossible, create eveningwear that is as beautiful as it is comfortable.

On her top tips for a stylish suitcase...
Packing in outfits does the trick for me. It’s useless having great separates and shoes in your luggage if none of them match. Also I make sure I take something I always feel comfortable in, just in case nothing else works. Since fashion week also means a lot of walking around and there is often no time to head back to the hotel and change, I like to take shoes that I know look good but I can also walk in. Paul Andrew and Tabitha Simmons are two of my favourite brands for this.

On the one piece in her wardrobe she’d never get rid of...
A thin gold bracelet my mother handed down to me. I wear it nearly every single day.

The secret to good style is…
...timelessness to me. Looking at pictures of icons like Ali Macgraw, Margaux Hemingway or Jean Shrimpton I am always amazed about how modern they look. Good style should outlast seasons and decades.
