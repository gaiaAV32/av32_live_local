BOLD AS BRASS

Our look-at-me jewellery brands

 

There’s been a revolutionary shift inside the jewellery box this season. Experimentation is rampant and everything is bigger and bolder than ever for statement’s sake. From fluid forms to confident colours and a marvellous range of metals and materials- here are the names you need to know:

 

Charlotte Chesnais

For French jewellery designer Charlotte Chesnais, shape is key to the wearable works of art she creates. Her pursuit for perfection allows sinuous streaks of metal to orbit fingers and wrists and dangle from ears flawlessly. The innovative forms change depending on the theme, which doesn’t dampen the show-stopping effect.

Lele Sadoughi

For American designer Lele Sadoughi, whose made her name with her blooming baubles, it’s the craftsmanship that counts. Inspired by her globetrotting adventures and love of modern art, life-like resin lily flowers with crystal centres are transformed into necklaces, rings and studs. These beautiful masterpieces are an original way to wear florals, and conversation-starters to boot.

Diana Broussard

If you’re after a cult classic, Diana Broussard’s Nate necklace surely fits the bill. Women worldwide can’t get enough of these clean and colourful chunky chains because there’s an urban edge to their inherent glamour. Crafted in Italy from plexiglass and resin, these matte necklaces elevate looks with modern pops of colour.

LEIVANKASH

Leila Kashanipour’s jewellery collections reflect her Persian roots and London home in exciting and abstract ways. ‘Oculus’ styles reimagine the evil eye motif graphically in various sizes for neck, wrist and finger using faux pearls and white howlite settings. London landmarks like The Shard and The London Eye inspired the angular lines.

Gabriele Frantzen

New York based German Gabriele Frantzen specialises in cheerful, everyday bracelets she calls ‘watch candy’. Rather than telling the time, colligate-striped straps are adorned with crystal faces and gold snakes instead. Ready to be layered and clashed, just like the streetstyle set are doing, they’re an easy way to personalise your style.

Anna e Alex

Best friends Anna Neri and Alessandra Sales bring the Italian passementerie technique back into the spotlight with a flurry of ornamental earrings. Two-toned rich braid is twisted, chandelier-like, and finished with semi-precious beads. Up to four inches long and much lighter than they look, these stunners exude high octane drama.
