PHILOSOPHY DI LORENZO SERAFINI

Imaginative Italian femininity

 

@philosophyofficial: The joy just after the Spring/Summer 2016 Fashion Show

 

In a season where romanticism and ruffles are rife, the scintillating sensuality that Lorenzo Serafini also conjures for Philosophy is all the more impressive. Wisps of silk and gauzy chiffon are transformed into fluid, flattering forms that look even prettier in motion. Inspired by the photography of David Hamilton, which the Italian designer first discovered as child, each piece is infused with a youthful serenity not seen elsewhere.

 

 

The details may be delicate but they’re more directional than you’d expect with vivid Toile de Jouys prints and dramatic black lace to break up the expected springtime palette of florals and pastels. Buttons and neck ties can be left undone, under camisoles are optional and styles switch easily between daywear and occasionwear.
Serafini is well-versed in this type of girlish-but-grownup glamour. He was lead womenswear designer at Dolce & Gabbana and Roberto Cavalli before taking the reins at Philosophy in 2014. It’s no wonder Alexa Chung and Alessandra Ambrosio have fallen hard for Philosophy di Lorenzo Serafini’s charms, it’s hard to resist them.
