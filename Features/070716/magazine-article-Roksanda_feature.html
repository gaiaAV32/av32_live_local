Here’s a dilemma most women will be familiar with: do you want to dress for men, or for women? Put it another way, do you opt for clothes which are flattering or fashion-forward? (This crude generalisation is a cliché for a reason). Well, ladies, Roksanda is the proof that you can have it all.

 

In the decade she’s helmed her eponymous label, designer Roksanda Ilincic has proved her knack for knowing what women really want to wear: clothes that tick both fashionable and flattering boxes. Inventive little twists and confident colour-blocking are hallmarks of her sculptural collections (those exaggerated silhouettes the legacy of her time studying architecture in her native Belgrade). Ilincic’s ageless aesthetic has won her industry acclaim and a loyal, varied fanbase; you’ll find two British Fashion Awards and two ELLE Style awards in her trophy cabinet, while the Duchess of Cambridge, Emma Stone and Naomi Harris are among those who’ve worn her designs.

‘I want to make clothes for women taking their child to school, running around during a busy day at work, and going out for dinner in the evening.’

The effortless ’70s-tinged shapes of Roksanda’s PRE FALL 16 collection are designed to be worn wherever and whenever. The curving sweep of waves and patchwork jacquards in contrasting colours run throughout, detailing a standout pair of wide culottes and a series of structured dresses that would look as good in the office as they would come cocktail hour. This season, the signature statement sleeves are more balloon than bell; while skin peeking through cut-outs and semi-slits offers a hint of sensuality. As ever, Ilincic proves her point with grace: looking good, doesn’t need to mean playing it safe.
