Australian brand macgraw is redefining what cool looks like. By subverting the femininity of their clean silhouettes with fun flourishes, founding sisters Beth and Tessa MacGraw have been making a captivating case for the appeal of pretty since launching their label in 2012. As romantic as the brand’s cult lace blouse is, for instance, it’s the wide bell sleeves which give it a contemporary edge (Pandora Sykes is a fan). Easily dressed up or down, macgraw clothes and glitter speckled shoes always make an ultra-stylish statement.
The sisters give us the inside track on the Pre Fall 16 collection, including their tips on how to style it. Feel free to take notes...

 

“This skirt shape has become a bit of a signature for us. We update it each season in our exclusive prints. I personally love wearing the Dressage skirt with a white tee or a cashmere knit and kicks.” Tess.

 

Where did you find inspiration for the shapes, colours and decorative details?
“Equestrienne” was born out of the heritage and romance of horse riding. We were quite drawn to the majesty and tradition; the beautiful layers and uniform tailoring. Once we started researching, we were inspired by a rich history of stoic equestriennes, from the fictional National Velvet to modern day Olympians and jockeys. Our prints (which are all hand drawn by Tessa) have become a collection highlight and we now create two prints each season, one of which is always a little more playful; enter this season’s horse print.

 

How do your collections fit into the brand’s ethical philosophy?
We make all the collections in Sydney and it is important for us to be connected to our production. We are very hands on with all our makers; they’re part of the team.

@macgrawlove: Beth and Tessa MacGraw

 

We love matching sets. We worked with a supplier to create our own scale and colours for this chevron lace. The weight of the fabric gives it a great drape.” Beth

How do you name your pieces?
Naming our collection is one of the really fun parts. We usually head to a bar or for dinner with a few creatives, discuss our inspiration, open a bottle of wine, and let the names flow. It’s a naming ceremony of sorts. You’ll see most of the names of pieces in this collection reference horse riding in some way: Canter, Jockey, Steeple.

 

Who is the macgraw woman?
Our girl is international and spirited; she doesn’t take herself too seriously but has a serious eye for detail.
