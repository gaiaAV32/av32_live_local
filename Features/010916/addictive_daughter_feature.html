Heart-broken? Stressed? Just feeling a bit ‘meh’? Persia Lawson and Joey Bradford can help

Today’s 20-somethings are feeling more stressed, depressed and all-round fed up than ever – so much for the time of your life. This was certainly the experience for friends Persia Lawson and Joey Bradford when, fresh out of drama school and not having the dream careers they’d imagined, they found themselves living chaotic lives and dating equally chaotic men. “We ended up spiralling down,” explains Joey. But this is not a cautionary tale so much as one of triumph in adversity. Turning to self-help books and therapy, the girls turned their lives around – and their “positive lifestyle movement”, Addictive Daughter, was born.
“We wanted to make spirituality and self-help accessible and aspirational for our generation,” explains Persia, “There’s a lot of value in it, but people don’t necessarily know about it, there’s stigma attached”. “Our aim is to package it in a way that speaks to young people in their terms,” echoes Joey. This means anything from lifecoaching sessions held via Skype to an online 21 Day Heart Rehab (heartbreak is, incidentally, the number one issue people come to them with); when we meet they have just returned from Wilderness where they hosted a workshop on how to have a spontaneous and connected festival experience. All is delivered with the same frank intimacy and humour of a close friend and with the same goal in mind: “We really believe self-worth is key,” says Persia. The girls share their ten tips for keeping that quarter life crisis at bay – however old you are.

1. If you want a drink

First ask yourself: Am I drinking to celebrate, or to escape? If it’s the latter, drinking’s probably more likely to leave you feeling worse off than before you imbibed, so perhaps consider what else you could do that may be more loving towards yourself.

2. If you’re feeling blue

Low energy? Slap a bit of red lippy on - it instantly lifts your mood. We once read somewhere that Parisians achieve that effortless glamour look by wearing red lippy with no other make-up - greatwhen you’re feeling lazy/in a hurry.

3. If you’re feeling overwhelmed

When you’re plagued by massive fear and anxiety over the future, remember to keep it in the day. Whilst it’s always good to have plans and visions for the future, focusing on what you need to do today, only, makes life feel far more manageable.

4. If you need to make an important decision

Best advice: If you don’t know, do nothing. It’s always better to wait until you feel calm, clear and certain, than to act impulsively and regret it later.

5. If you want to cultivate a positive relationship with money

Commit to using a portion of what you have to help and support someone/ something else you believe in - even if that’s just £3 a month to a charity that does great work. Giving is the most effective way to feel useful and empowered.

6. If you’re feeling judgemental

Accept people for who they are, and where they are in life. It’s never nice to feel judged by someone else, so make it your mission to be compassionate and respectful of other people’s opinions and choices.

7. If you’re feeling scared

Feeling like your life isn’t matching up to where you feel you should be at this point? Remember this mantra: “I’m exactly where I’m meant to be in this moment, and if I surrender to it and trust then I’ll be gently carried towards the happiest and healthiest circumstances for myself and my life.”

8. If the grass always looks greener elsewhere

The best medicine for the compare and despair syndrome that stems from social media stalking is gratitude. Not everything you see on someone’s profile and newsfeed tells the whole story, so don’t be fooled. Stop looking over your neighbour’s fence and coveting their green grass, nurture and be grateful for the grass on your own side.

9. If there’s tension in a relationship

Whenever difficulties or resentment crop up in a friendship or relationship, ask yourself: How important is it? Life really is too short to allow ourselves to feel crappy about things that don’t really matter, so make it your daily practice to forgive, forget and move on.

10. If you focus on what’s inside

As we share in our book ‘The Inner Fix’, do this, and the outsides will take care of themselves. The reason we tend to want the best car, flat, boyfriend or job is in order to feel good on the inside, so, if that’s the objective, then surely it makes more sense to try and feel good on the inside first? Meditation is an excellent way of helping you work on your internal relationship with yourself - we absolutely swear by it.
