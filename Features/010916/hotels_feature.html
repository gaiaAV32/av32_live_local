DESIGN DESTINATIONS

 

From Corbusier furniture to Helmut Newtons on the walls and expertly curated libraries, meet the new breed of hotels so stylish that design mavens are in danger of never leaving.

 
Any pretender can put an Eames chair in a hotel lobby but which design hotels really merit the name? Whether a jewel box in the middle of it all or a far out retreat complete with every luxury, there are some very special places out there. Explore our selection of the most thoughtfully curated, aesthetically pleasing design spaces well worth a visit.
 
 

Hotel Grand Amour, Paris

Housed in a pre-Haussmannian building on rue de la Fidélité, Swedish graffiti artist André Saraiva’s second hotel is a delicious combination of naughty and nice. Linked by a characteristic penis-print carpet by André and Pierre Frey (ooh la la), each of the 44 rooms are unique and include libraries curated by provocateur Olivier Zahm and fashion writer Glenn O’Brien and works of art by Guy Bourdin, Carlotta Kohl and Helmut Newton. All are filled with vintage furniture and Hermès amenities. A veritable feast for the eyes, there are no TVs to be found.

Hotel Alexandra, Copenhagen

Decorated in ’50s and ’60s style with museum-quality furniture, Hotel Alexandra is a haven of mid-century Danish design. Gems include the Verner Panton Suite - a celebration of the furniture designer’s iconic work and Christian Louboutin’s room of choice when in town. The ’70s-inspired Collector’s Suite, re-envisioned every three months by gallerist and collector Anders Peterson with general manager Jeppe Muhlhausen, features pieces by H. J. Wegner, Adam Hoff and Hans Olsen, with most of the interior available to purchase.

Elma Arts Complex, Israel

The result of eight years of extensive planning and construction, Yaakov Rechter’s award-winning Brutalist masterpiece the Mivtachim Sanitarium reopened this year as the Elma Arts Complex. Owned by philanthropist and art collector Lily Elstein, it is destined to be a major creative hub. The exposed concrete, clean lines and unadorned geometrical shapes overlook vast sloping forests and the Mediterranean, and house concert halls, expansive galleries and artists’ studios. Feeling creative? Don’t miss the interactive master classes with dancers, musicians, actors and authors.

The Williamsburg Hotel, New York

Opening earlier this year in Brooklyn, 150-room The Williamsburg Hotel boasts epic, 10-ft views of the Manhattan skyline – the first of many treats. Staff are outfitted by CFDA/Vogue Fashion Fund finalist Ovadia & Sons, furnishings custom designed by Michaelis Boyd Studio (whose portfolio includes the Groucho Club and Soho House Berlin) and toiletries handmade in Brooklyn by Apotheke. Did we mention there’s also a rooftop pool, and a 3,400 sq ft ballroom?

The Qvest, Cologne

A former historic city archive, The Qvest Hotel is a mid-century and Bauhaus aficionado’s dream, housed in a heritage-listed Gothic building created after a design by Friedrich Carl Heimann. Furnished largely by Qvest magazine owner Michael Kaune’s personal collection of furniture and photographs, the result is a veritable design museum where you’ll spot pieces by Arne Jacobsen, Corbusier and Mies van der Rohe. Don’t miss the exclusive curated shop and the small library of art, design and fashion literature replacing TVs in each room.
 
 
SHOP NOW >
 
 

Mar Adentro Mexico

This all-white 205-room retreat in calm, creative San Jose del Cabo on the Bajan peninsula is a minimalist triumph, facing the ocean and framed by faux canals and saltwater pools. Designed and owned by renowned Mexican architect Miguel Angel Aragones, the hotel is set apart by its specialist art and music program - its own art gallery with rotating local and international exhibitions, a fashion partnership with designer Carla Fernandez and an artist-in-residence partnership with LA’s Hotel Café.
