If you thought you had Dodo Bar Or pinned down, think again. After a summer collection of showstopping keffiyeh-inspired maxi dresses, the Israeli designer’s directive for the new season is a celebration of sweetie wrapper metallics, retro cool and disco fever. In a word: Lurex. Created in 1947 the yarn incorporates shimmering metallic fibres, giving the material an irresistible metallic, starry-night appearance. Trademarked (such was the level of innovation and the revolution it unleashed), the yarn is coated with a polyamide film so that the metal’s shine does not tarnish. The fabric fast became a staple for designers looking to infuse their designs with glitz and glamour – that said, you’ll find these spirited threads surprisingly easy to work into your wardrobe.

How to make Lurex work on a 24 timeline? Reserve the top-to-toe full looks for a night out on the tiles and a skirt or top paired with neutral separates for an elevated, day-friendly ensemble. If ever there was a time to invoke the desk to dancing cliché (in the best possible way), this is it.
