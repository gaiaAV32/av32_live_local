SALE SHOPPING WITH PANDORA SYKES

Let the Wardrobe Mistress guide you

 

Sale time can be overwhelming for even the most self-assured shoppers. In such times of sartorial indecision we often find ourselves thinking: what would Pandora Sykes do? Sure, thanks to her fearless, fun approach to getting dressed the fashion journalist has catapulted herself to front row favourite, but (as a cursory glance at her Instagram feed will attest) Sykes really is that stylish ‘off duty’ too. Thankfully for us, she also well versed in doling out indispensable fashion advice, as demonstrated in both in her Sunday Times Style Wardrobe Mistress column and on her website, pandorasykes.com. So we asked her to select and style her favourite pieces from our SS16 sale. Watch and learn.

 
 

A.W.A.K.E
Blue Gingham Cotton Frill Dress

 

AWAKE

There are two reasons I love A.W.A.K.E: firstly, because the acronym stands for All Wonderful Adventures Kindle Enthusiasm (how can you not love a label named that?) and secondly, because the silhouettes are so unexpected. Who'd have thought a gingham midi dress could be so directional? The one arm in, one arm out thing is going to throw some people, which only makes me want to wear it more. The ruffle will tempt them all back anyway. Wear to summer parties for ever and ever. Amen.

 
 

ROSETTA GETTY Terracotta Tie-Front Wrap Dress

 

Rosetta Getty

I stopped wondering why Rosetta Getty was pricey the moment I put this dress on: the draping. Oh, the DRAPING! Getty knows what she is doing. I also love the terracotta colour. I’d never gravitate towards a dress like this, but it looks amazing next to gold. And it flares quite wonderfully when you walk. Also works with a white t-shirt underneath, if you love it too much to save it for smart occasions.

 
 

COURREGES
Red Bicolour Cap Sleeve Dress
RACHEL COMEY
White Leather Block Heel Shoes

 

Courreges

Put your hand up if you're excited that Courrèges is back. This dress is actually entirely leather - which I didn't realise until I had it in my hot little hand - so you could feasibly wear it in winter, too, with a poloneck underneath making it a year-round dress, something I'm always a fan of. I love the bright colours; so ’60s when worn with white shoes and ginormous earrings.
